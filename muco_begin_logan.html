History

Belgium, one of Europe’s earliest industrial powers, gained independence from the Netherlands in 1830 after centuries under Burgundian and later Habsburg rule, saw its medieval cities like Bruges flourish as major trade centers, and endured pivotal battles in both World War I and World War II, shaping its complex and influential place in European history.

Fun facts about Belgium

  • Belgium produces over 1,500 types of beer.
  • It’s the world’s biggest exporter of chocolate.
  • The Saxophone was invented in Belgium by Adolphe Sax.
  • Belgium has more castles per square mile than any other country.
  • Brussels Airport is the #1 chocolate-selling point on Earth.

Belgian National Team

Facts

The Belgium national football team, known as the Red Devils and governed by the Royal Belgian Football Association, has featured a “Golden Generation” of stars like Eden Hazard, Kevin De Bruyne, Romelu Lukaku, and Thibaut Courtois, achieved a FIFA world ranking of #1 multiple times, finished third in the 2018 World Cup, second in Euro 1980, plays home matches at King Baudouin Stadium, and is renowned for its iconic red kit and world-class players competing in top European leagues.
